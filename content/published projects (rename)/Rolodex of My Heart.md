Rolodex of the Heart is an interactive installation of found objects, digitally fabricated paper and wood materials, and prints of personal photo archives. Viewers explore the interplay between evolving connections through a facilitated ritual of introspection within the tactile décor of a bedroom-office.

The installation explores the playfulness of cultivating art-focused relationships. The play contains duality: both sudden, unpredictable storylines and lasting, intimate impact on my heart and mind.

The fast-paced energy of city life results in constantly evolving connections–both social and artistic. After years of living in my mind in earnest and disarray, it was time to map these connections. With emotional and creative influence in mind, I built an interaction that mirrors the entanglements that come from having made these connections.

My accumulated archive of digital photographs of these relationships is the seed used to conceptualize the juxtaposition between messy love and the practicalities of connection.

At the center of the installation is a desk, anchoring a transformed bedroom-office space: a space in my life I use for daily creation, reflection, and archiving. On the desk rests a box inspired by a Rolodex, though physically operating more like a filing cabinet in this installation. Its moving parts and hand-fabricated details echo the rhythms of navigating a city across the span of a week. 

The box (made of found cardboard, wood, and upholstery linen material) contains alphabetized cards (made of drawing paper)and tabs (made of laser-cut scrap wood) that fit and adhere to the card’s edge. 

Each cards contains photographs pertaining to the individual that is archived on the card. Handwritten biographical notes appear on the cards along with witty captions on the backs of select prints that recall shared memories. These contributions serve as poetic data points, revealing the artistic identities of those I love while highlighting the quirks of our connections.

The photos (printed on cardstock) capture moments that span from art collaborations to disco-filled nights in the city. Each image, no larger than two by two inches, originates from my personal cloud archive. This process resists the risk of digital storage, where images are often pushed from the forefront of my emotional center to the backburner of disengaged data, identities, and spaces.

When viewers engage with the work, the tactile ritual of shuffling through the photos inside the sliding folders, is intended to evoke the sporadic way memories and individuals resurface in my conversations. The ritual is further complemented by clothespins and gold metal tesseract cubes. 

The cubes are a set I stumbled upon in my neighborhood, much like the manner that helped form the same connections that are featured in this piece. These cubes are suspended from the wall and ceiling, recalling the eclectic yet functional décor of my real bedroom—the setting where much of the work that fuels these connections takes place.

Clothespins are available to attach to the cubes, acting as a tool to hang the images. This allows images to be spread out and reorganized into patterns across the connections.

Rolodex of the Heart is an ongoing conversation about how the messiness of building connections as an artist in the city pays off—amplifying both the spontaneity of living with relationships and the deliberate practice of archiving.
