# Background

On a breezy but walkable Thursday in March 2024, I was making my way up 5th Avenue in Park Slope with the agenda of getting groceries before class. I serendipously walk into Nails Salon, not recognizing the place of business even though I have lived in an around the neighborhood for the better part of five years. 

Inside were two lovely humans. One turned out to share a mutual with me from my program at NYU. The other, Billy Merrell, was the owner of this repurposed nail salon turned exposed-wood gallery. 

Just seconds before I take off to have time to pick out some aspargus before my evening class on Jay St, Billy offers me his business card in a fashion akin to how I explore relationships in [[Rolodex of My Heart|the gallery piece I was currently wrapping up]].

# Theme Blurb

There is love and there is ease in consciously turning to what a human knows: the sun rising, the moon phasing from quarter to full.  However, at times, the human misses these turns and instead stumbles over waves of unpredictable emotion;  over a shopping cart of domestic chaos; and over a battlefield of fights that reveal the depths of an irrational society.   

When we drown in these difficulties the lifeboat can come in the form of a flame that rekindles our way of acting and being by turning the mind back to soothing knowledge.

Seeking Conscious Comfort is this reminder–a collection of poetic imagery that exists as affirmation. Namely, of the intrinsic numerous ways the power of the Human Being spins a web of comfort on this earnest Earth.

As the multifaceted climate today reacts to all the many things we can even comprehend, we–the artists–protest, protect, and enact a sacred form of Pride in which we seep into wonder, not into wallow; in which we bask in the beauty and not mask through conformity. These artworks weave a snapshot into the collective spiritual responsibility to raise the standards of human experience with simple fortitude and familiar love.
