Avital Rimon (she/her) is a multimedia artist, storyteller, curator, and events producer dwelling in New York City.  Her stories are her vehicle, driven by inquiry and further propelled by the use of reflexive narrative. She leans heavily on writing  in the process of prototyping and post mortem, she develops projects she selectively utilizes audio stories, assembled manual and lasercut fabrications, drawing, IoT, site specific production design, and accessible ritualistic interactivity.

Her goal is to follow the succinct words of  Martin L. Gore of Depeche Mode: "see the microcosm in macro vision." She practices art to invoke change in ways that extend beyond a utilitarian approach. By giving her boiling menace an outlet in the process, the general practitioner within her cultivates space to draw attention to morbidities, meditate on healing properties, and bring about mending.

A child of late Soviet-era Jewish immigrants and an immigrant herself, Avital has a deep adoration and nostalgia to the culture her parents were raised on and used to raise her, specifically Soviet children's cinema.
