I am a multimedia artist, storyteller, curator, and events producer dwelling in New York City.  My stories are her vehicle, driven by inquiry and further propelled by the use of reflexive narrative. I lean heavily on writing  in the process of prototyping and post mortem, I develop projects utilize audio stories, assembled manual and lasercut fabrications, drawing, IoT, site-specific production design, and accessible ritualistic interactivity.

My goal is to practice art that invokes change in ways that extend beyond the limits of a utilitarian approach. Or, quoting a Depeche Mode lyric, my goal more succinctly is to "see the microcosm in the macro vision."  By giving my boiling menace an outlet in the process, the general practitioner within me builds an inlet to draw attention to morbidities, meditate on healing properties, and bring about mending.

A child of late Soviet-era Jewish immigrants and an immigrant herself, I have a deep adoration and nostalgia to the culture her parents were raised on and used to raise me, specifically Soviet children's cinema. 

Production methods I use include the drawing skills I've built since being able to chicken scratch with a fork, fabrication rooted in my robotics and drawing in studio art in her teens, studying computation at Carnegie Mellon, and receiving a BFA in Interactive Media Arts at NYU Tisch. Like a sentient branch on a tree that simply said, "how can I be derivative--I want more," my grit plays into her art through the encompassing process of inquiring on pre-existing inquiries.

In 2025 she worked with galleries including participating in a group show at The Blanc in NYC with her interactive installation Rolodex of My Heart in an NYU group show "Out of Storage." She has curated the show Seeking Conscious Comfort, a show within a series of four Pride Month shows titled Many Rooms: One House. This was a two-site gallery group show exhibited at  Nails Salon and Gallery 198 in Brooklyn. It was a collection of poetic imagery on the intrinsic and numerous ways the power of the Human Being spins a web of comfort on this earnest Earth.

Following a similar theme is my poem, "On Simplicity", whixch has been accepted in the *Resistance Anthology* that is curated and edited by Angel Rosen and Joshua Pitkin. It is expected to be published in the coming year.
